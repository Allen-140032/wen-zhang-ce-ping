# Scoring Rules

This project uses a practical editorial checklist for WeChat public account articles. The current MVP focuses on transparent heuristics rather than hidden ranking claims.

Each article is scored across six dimensions. Each dimension returns 1-10 points, for a total of 60.

## 1. Title Appeal

Checks whether the title gives readers a clear reason to click.

Signals:

- Specific result, number, contrast, question, pain point, or curiosity gap.
- Clear subject and audience.
- Avoids being too generic or purely decorative.

## 2. Opening Hook

Checks whether the first screen quickly creates attention.

Signals:

- Starts with a problem, conflict, concrete result, story, or counter-intuitive claim.
- Avoids long background setup.
- Makes the reader understand why the article matters.

## 3. Paragraph Rhythm

Checks whether the article is comfortable to read on mobile.

Signals:

- Short paragraphs.
- Clear transitions.
- Useful headings or list structure.
- Avoids dense blocks of text.

## 4. Emotional Curve

Checks whether the article has tension, turns, and payoff.

Signals:

- Moves from problem to pressure to resolution.
- Contains at least one emotional high point.
- Gives readers a reason to share or remember the piece.

## 5. Readability

Checks whether the article is easy to understand.

Signals:

- Plain language.
- Limited jargon or explained technical terms.
- Sentence length suitable for mobile reading.

## 6. Call to Action

Checks whether the article gives readers a clear next step.

Signals:

- Encourages follow, save, comment, share, try a tool, or continue reading.
- The action is connected to the article's value.
- Avoids vague or forced conversion language.

## Current limitations

- The MVP is an editorial helper, not an objective popularity predictor.
- Scores are intended for draft review and revision tracking.
- Real-world performance still depends on audience, timing, distribution, and account trust.
